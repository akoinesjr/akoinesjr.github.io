Emotional Conflict

Housemate Struggles

1. Discuss a time you had an emotional conflict with someone. What was it about? What happened?

I lived in a house with five roommates while in college, and this experience in volved quite a bit of conflict. All of my other experiences with roommates have been quite positive, but living with this group had some significant difficulties.

2. What was the source of the conflict?

At the beginning of the year, we all got along quite well, and we had a large group of mutual friends who lived outside the house who would come to visit somewhat regularly. Our house had two floors. I lived downstairs with two of my friends, and three of our other friends lived upstairs. The upstairs roommates had a bit of a falling out with some of our other friends who lived outside the house, and this created quite a bit of tension as they no longer wanted them around, but we didn't feel that was fair.

3. How did you handle that conflict?

Not particularly well. We stopped communicating as well, and organized sharing of housework completely collapsed, which resulted in a much messier house, and by extension, frustrated housemates both upstairs and downstairs.

4. Did your actions make the conflict better or worse?

I'm not sure that I did a lot to make things worse, but I certainly could have done things to make it better. Looking at the "Compass of Shame", I believe most of us fell into avoidance, some of us into withdrawal, and some even into attacking others.

5. If you could go back, what would you do differently, if anything?

I would have make an effort to engage the group in rational discussions to find a solution.

6. What did you learn from this experience?

Communication is key. If you are not willing to work with someone to solve a problem, you are never going to fix it.
